Rules of Engagement
What belongs in QBR
Strategic ops story
Initiative ROI, pipeline health, customer metrics, org development, hiring asks. Forward-looking. Gary sets the narrative.
What doesn't belong
Hard finance & P&L
Revenue actuals, gross margin, balance sheet, SG&A — that's the finance team's pack. Reference it, don't own it. Never duplicate it.
How to frame the ask
Data first, ask second
Every budget or headcount request must be preceded by a metric that justifies it. "CRM coverage is 1.8× — we need a Sales Lead to fix that" lands better than just asking.
Tone with the president
Ops partner, not reporter
Don't just present numbers — bring a point of view. A finance background respects directness and conviction over slides full of data without a recommendation.
Targets & baselines
Set in Month 1, then trend
First QBR establishes the baseline. Variance trends matter more than point-in-time numbers. Direction of travel is the story — are things getting better?
Welbilt / Ali Group readiness
Build the story now
Every QBR is practice for the parent org. By Q3–Q4 Gary will have clean data showing the operational transformation he's led. That's the story that matters upward.

How We Use AI — Three Principles
01
AI amplifies people, doesn't replace them
Every AI initiative here frees a team member to do higher-value work — it doesn't eliminate their role. Gabria approves what AI drafts. Brian handles what the IVA can't. Gary interprets what the dashboard surfaces.
02
Discover before you automate
The IVA and CS triage tools are deliberately scoped after the CS discovery sprint. Automating a broken process makes a broken process faster — not better. Discovery first, build second, always.
03
Measure the productivity gain
Every AI initiative has a measurable outcome: deflection rate, quote accuracy, response time, reporting hours saved. These are QBR metrics. If we can't measure the gain, we can't justify the build to the president.
